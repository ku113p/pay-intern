# Competitor Analysis: Try-Before-Hire Models

## Overview

Companies that use paid trial periods as part of their hiring process.
These are internal hiring processes, not platforms -- but they validate the model
DevStage aims to productize.

## Automattic (WordPress.com)

- **Model**: Every hire goes through a paid trial project (2-8 weeks)
- **Track record**: 2,500+ hires over a decade using this method
- **Compensation**: Paid at $25/hour during trial
- **Process**: Apply -> trial project -> evaluation -> offer
- **Result**: High retention, strong culture fit

**Key insight**: Automattic proved that paid work trials are the best predictor
of job success at scale. But their model is:
- Internal only (not a platform)
- One-way (only Automattic evaluates)
- Resource-intensive (each trial demands heavy team investment)
- One candidate at a time, 70+ hours invested per candidate

## Linear

- **Model**: Paid contract project as final interview stage
- **Duration**: Typically 2-4 weeks
- **Result**: <50% post-trial rejection rate; 96% retention for those who pass
- **Scale**: Small team, each trial is highly personalized

**Key insight**: Linear's 96% retention proves the model works, but it
doesn't scale beyond a small company's hiring needs.

## Zapier

- **Model**: Paid trial project for many roles
- **Philosophy**: "The best signal is actual work in the actual role"
- **Result**: Used successfully for remote-first distributed team

## Gumroad

- **Model**: Paid project-based trial for all engineering hires
- **Philosophy**: No traditional interviews; just work together

## PostHog

- **Model**: Paid "SuperDay" -- a full working day with the team
- **Philosophy**: Experience the actual work environment

## What They Prove

| Metric | Traditional Hiring | Paid Trial Model |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| Retention rate | Varies widely | 96% (Linear) |
| Prediction accuracy | 57% (interviews) | Much higher (work = work) |
| Candidate experience | 60% drop-off | Engaged throughout |
| Culture fit assessment | Gut feeling | Observed over weeks |
| Skill verification | Resume + test | Real output |

## Why This Hasn't Become a Platform (Until DevStage)

### The Barriers DevStage Removes

1. **Infrastructure cost**: Each company must build its own trial program.
   DevStage provides the platform infrastructure.

2. **Process design**: Companies don't know how to structure trials.
   DevStage's outcome criteria provide structure.

3. **Scale**: Internal trials handle one candidate at a time.
   DevStage enables parallel listings and applications.

4. **Standardization**: Each company's trial is unique and non-portable.
   DevStage outcome reviews create a portable, standardized format.

5. **Accessibility**: Only well-known companies can attract trial candidates.
   DevStage provides a marketplace where any company can participate.

6. **Bidirectionality**: Internal trials are always company-initiated.
   DevStage lets developers post listings too.
