# Pain Point: Resume Fraud in Tech

## Summary

The tech industry has the highest resume fraud rate of any sector. 55% of IT employees
admit to falsification, and AI-powered tools are making deception increasingly sophisticated.

## Scale of the Problem

### Overall Statistics
- **55%** of IT employees admit to resume falsification (Crosschq 2025) -- highest of any sector
- **64.2%** of Americans have lied on resumes at least once (up from 55% in 2022)
- **7 in 10 workers** admitted to lying on resumes (ResumeLab 2023, 1,900 respondents)
- **86%** of U.S. employers have caught an applicant lying (HireRight)
- **42%** of resume fraudsters lied on **every single resume** they submitted
- **6 in 10** resume fraudsters successfully landed a job in 2024

### What They Lie About
- Altering dates of positions: **50%**
- Exaggerating responsibilities/experience: **32%**
- Falsifying job titles: **18%**
- Lying about AI-related skills: **11%**
- **60%** of candidates claim mastery in skills they have only basic knowledge of

### Financial Impact
- Resume fraud costs U.S. businesses an estimated **$600 billion annually** (ACFE)
- ~$4,500 per employee

> Sources: Crosschq 2025; Resume.org 2024; AMS Inform; ResumeBuilder

## The Resume Paradox

Resumes are simultaneously the most used and least reliable hiring tool:

- When recruiters predicted candidates had **0-5% chance** of passing, those candidates
  actually **passed 47% of the time**
- Resumes measure **writing skills more than technical ability**
- **85 years of research** show resumes are "entirely ineffective at predicting job performance"
- A candidate can list the right tech stack and **still struggle with basics**
- The "years of experience" metric is called a "toxic, counterproductive myth" by industry experts

> Sources: eSkill; Recruiting Toolbox; CodingHorror; Daily.dev

## The Deepfake Dimension

Remote hiring has opened new fraud vectors:
- **17%** of hiring managers encountered suspected deepfake interviews by end of 2024
  (up from 3% -- a 500% increase in 12 months)
- **95%** of organizations experienced a deepfake incident in the past year (HYPR 2025)
- A job applicant can be deepfaked into existence in **70 minutes**
- Companies lose an average of **$28,000** per proxy hire detection incident
- **Gartner predicts**: by 2028, **1 in 4 candidate profiles** will be entirely fake

### North Korean State-Sponsored Fraud
- Over **300 American companies** unknowingly hired North Korean-linked impostors
- Used stolen U.S. identities
- Included defense contractors and automakers
- 14 DPRK nationals indicted in December 2024

## Why DevStage Makes Resume Fraud Irrelevant

When developers are evaluated through real project work with measurable outcome criteria,
resume lies become meaningless:

| Fraud Type | Traditional Hiring | DevStage |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| Inflated skills | Undetected until after hire | Exposed during real work |
| Fake experience | Caught too late (if ever) | Irrelevant -- real output is measured |
| AI-assisted cheating | Growing epidemic | Can't cheat real project delivery |
| Proxy interviews | 10-15% of remote interviews | Work happens over weeks, not hours |
| Deepfake identity | Increasingly sophisticated | Extended engagement reveals truth |
