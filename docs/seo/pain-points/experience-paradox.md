# Pain Point: The Experience Paradox

## Summary

Junior developers face an unprecedented structural crisis: entry-level jobs require
experience they can't get, and the traditional pathways (internships, campus recruiting,
training programs) have collapsed.

## The Paradox

```
"Requires 3+ years of experience"
        |
        v
Can't get a job without experience
        |
        v
Can't get experience without a job
        |
        v
(repeat forever)
```

## The Data

### Entry-Level Job Requirements Have Inflated
- **35-38%** of "entry-level" jobs require 3+ years of experience (LinkedIn, 3.8M postings)
- In software/manufacturing: **exceeds 50%**
- Share of tech jobs requiring <=3 years fell from **43% (2018) to 28% (2024)** (IEEE Spectrum)
- Share requiring 5+ years rose from **37% to 42%** (Q2 2022-Q2 2025)

### True Entry-Level Has Vanished
- True entry-level roles: only **~2.5%** of all tech job postings (early 2024)
- Entry-level developer jobs **vanished by 67%** between 2023-2024 (Stanford)
- Listings down **~70% from their 2022 peak** (Indeed/FRED)
- New graduates were **32% of Big Tech hires** in 2019; only **7% by 2026** (78% reduction)

### The Bait-and-Switch
- "Entry-level software engineer" postings **grew 47%** (Oct 2023-Nov 2024)
- But **actual hiring** into those levels **dropped 73%**
- Companies advertise junior roles, then fill them with experienced engineers

> Sources: Stanford Digital Economy Lab; IEEE Spectrum; Indeed Hiring Lab; ADP

### Companies Cutting Junior Pipelines
- **Salesforce** halted junior hiring entirely for 2025
- **Google and Meta** hiring ~50% fewer new graduates vs 2021
- UK entry-level tech roles fell **46%** in 2024
- Indian IT services reduced entry-level roles by **20-25%** (EY)

## The AI Excuse

- **37%** of employers say they'd rather "hire" AI than a recent graduate
- **70%** of hiring managers believe AI can do the jobs of interns
- **57%** trust AI's work more than interns' or recent grads' work
- One senior engineer + AI = output of **three 2020-era juniors**
- BUT: Stanford data shows actual AI productivity gains are only **2.1%**,
  not the 24.69% executives believe. AI is a convenient excuse for cuts
  driven by high interest rates.

## Impact on Developers

### Career Statistics
- CS graduate unemployment: **6.1%** (2025) -- higher than fine arts
- **41-42.5%** underemployment among recent graduates
- Average job search: **6 months, 200+ applications**
- Employment for software developers aged 22-25 **declined nearly 20%** from 2022 peak
- Nearly **1 in 4** recent CS graduates is unemployed or in non-technical roles

### Internship Pipeline Collapse
- Internship listings fell **30%** since 2023 (Handshake)
- Tech internships: **273 applications** per posting (up from 161)
- Only **62%** of interns receive full-time offers -- lowest in 5+ years
- Only **50%** of recent graduates participated in any internship

### Mental Health
- **68%** report "extended silence" from employers
- **60%+** report "overwhelming anxiety" related to career prospects
- **34%** remain concerned about layoffs even during recovery

## The Pipeline Destruction Problem

Today's juniors are tomorrow's senior engineers:
- **67% hiring cliff** in 2024-2026 = **67% fewer potential leaders** in 2031-2036
- U.S. projected shortage: **1.2 million software engineers by 2026**
- By 2032: **60-70% reduction** in qualified tech leads and architects
- BLS projects **15% growth** in developer employment 2024-2034

The industry is eating its seed corn.

## How DevStage Breaks the Loop

DevStage creates a new pathway that bypasses the paradox entirely:

1. **Developer finds listings** with clear outcome criteria
2. **Applies based on motivation** and learning goals, not years of experience
3. **Works on real project** with a real company for a defined duration
4. **Receives structured outcome review** (pass/partial/fail per criterion)
5. **Uses verified outcomes** as proof of ability for future opportunities

No experience required to start. Real experience gained by the end.
