# Pain Point: The Collapse of the Internship Pipeline

## Summary

Traditional internship programs -- the primary pathway for junior developers to gain
experience -- have contracted sharply since 2023, with tech internships becoming the
most competitive category at 273 applications per posting.

## The Decline in Numbers

### Posting Volume
- Internship listings fell **more than 15%** from January 2023 to January 2025 overall
- **Technology-specific** internship postings dropped **30%** since 2023 (Handshake)
- Professional services postings dropped **42%**
- Internship postings on Handshake have been **down double digits each of the last three years**
- Overall intern hiring expected to fall an additional **3.1%**

### Competition Has Exploded
- Average applications per internship: **109** in 2024-2025 (up from 62 in 2023-2024, 43 in 2022-2023)
- **Technology sector**: **273 applications per posting** (up from 161 the prior year)
- This is nearly **2x more competitive** than the year before

### Conversion Rates Are Falling
- Employers extended fewer full-time offers: **62% offer rate** -- lowest in 5+ years
- Only **half** of recent graduates participated in any internship while enrolled
- First-generation students: only 50% had internship access vs. 66% for peers

> Sources: Handshake 2025; NACE Internship Report; CNBC; HR Dive

## Why It's Happening

1. **AI displacement narrative** -- companies believe AI can replace intern-level work
2. **Cost cutting** -- internship programs are expensive to run (mentorship, infrastructure)
3. **Remote work** -- harder to mentor interns in distributed teams
4. **Reduced hiring** -- overall tech hiring pullback hits entry-level hardest
5. **Over-supply** -- CS graduates doubled while positions halved

## The Downstream Effect

Without internships, the talent pipeline breaks:

```
No internships available
     |
     v
Graduates have no experience
     |
     v
Companies require experience for "entry-level"
     |
     v
Graduates can't get hired
     |
     v
Industry loses future senior engineers and tech leads
     |
     v
Senior shortage in 5-10 years
```
