# Pain Point: The Cost of a Bad Hire

## Summary

A bad developer hire costs $150,000-$300,000 when all factors are included.
74% of employers admit to having made at least one bad hire.

## Direct Cost Estimates

- **U.S. Department of Labor**: 30% of first-year earnings (~$40K-$54K for a developer)
- **CareerBuilder**: 74% of employers made bad hires; average reported cost: $14,900
- **Toggl Hire 2025**: direct costs $5K-$10K, indirect costs balloon to $30K-$150K+
- **C-level executives estimate**: ~$50,000 per failed hire
- **Comprehensive estimates**: $240,000-$850,000 per bad hire (all factors included)
- **Software engineering specifically**: $150,000-$300,000 (including productivity loss,
  senior engineer compensation, and full replacement cycle)

> Sources: U.S. DOL; CareerBuilder 2024; Toggl Hire 2025; Apollo Technical; DistantJob

## Frequency

- **23%** of companies report up to 5 bad hires per year
- At $17,000 each (conservative): $85,000/year in losses from hiring mistakes alone
- **74%** of employers have made at least one bad hire (CareerBuilder)
- **Nearly 60%** of bad hires occurred because the employee could not produce
  the level of work required

## Hidden Cost Multipliers

### Team Impact
- **30%** reduction in team morale following a bad hire
- Often triggers attrition among top performers
- Managers spend **20-30%** of their time over months dealing with a bad hire
- Remaining team members must compensate, leading to burnout

### Turnover Compounding
- Replacing an employee costs **1.5-2x annual salary** (Josh Bersin)
- For a $130K developer: $195,000-$260,000 replacement cost
- Tech sector average turnover: **13.2%**
- In a 100-person company, 10% turnover costs ~$700,000/year

### Early Attrition (Onboarding Failure)
- **20%** of turnover occurs within first 45 days (SHRM)
- **33%** of new hires look for a new job within first 6 months
- **20.5%** of HR leaders report up to half of new hires leave within 90 days
- Top reason: "misalignment between job expectations and reality" (30.3%)
- Only **12%** of employees strongly agree their organization does great onboarding

## Why Bad Hires Happen

1. **Resume fraud** -- 55% of IT employees lie (see [Resume Fraud](resume-fraud.md))
2. **Interview theater** -- interviews test presentation skills, not work skills
3. **Overconfidence** -- hiring managers systematically overestimate their judgment
4. **Time pressure** -- rushing to fill positions leads to compromised standards
5. **AI cheating** -- candidates use unauthorized tools (see [AI Cheating](ai-cheating-interviews.md))

## How DevStage Prevents Bad Hires

The "try before you hire" model directly addresses the root causes:

| Cause | Traditional Hiring | DevStage |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Resume lies | Detected after hire (if ever) | Irrelevant -- real work is measured |
| Interview performance != job performance | High correlation gap | No gap -- same context |
| Cultural misfit | Discovered in first weeks | Discovered during engagement |
| Skill mismatch | Costly to discover | Outcome criteria expose immediately |
| Expectation misalignment | #1 cause of early departure | Both sides experience reality first |

**Key insight**: Early attrition's #1 cause is "misalignment between expectations and reality."
DevStage eliminates this by making reality the evaluation method.
