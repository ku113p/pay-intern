# Pain Point: Why Traditional Interviews Don't Work

## Summary

85 years of research show that traditional interviews are barely better than a coin flip
at predicting job performance. The interview process is also so long and complex that
60% of candidates abandon it.

## Predictive Validity

### The Numbers
- Unstructured interviews predict success **~57% of the time** -- barely better than chance
- Meta-analytic validity coefficient: **.37** (on a 0-1 scale)
- 95% credibility interval: **-.07 to .77** -- interviews sometimes have *negative* predictive value
- **"Years of experience"** and **"first impressions"** are among the worst predictors
  of actual job performance
- **85 years of research** show resumes are "entirely ineffective at predicting job performance"

> Sources: Schmidt & Hunter 1998 meta-analysis; Sackett et al. 2022;
> eSkill; Recruiting Toolbox

### The Expert Illusion
- Hiring managers systematically overestimate their intuitive judgment (Scott Highhouse, I-O psychologist)
- Interview performance measures **communication in a high-stakes social setting**,
  not ability to manage workload, ambiguity, or sustained pressure over time
- Confirmation bias: interviewers form opinions in first 10 seconds, then seek confirming evidence

## Process Complexity

### How Many Rounds
- **50%+** of employers put candidates through 4+ rounds over 4-6 weeks
- Typical software engineer process: 3 stages (HR screen, tech screen, final rounds)
- Total candidate time: **5-7+ hours** minimum
- Big tech (Microsoft): 4-5 rounds, 3-7 weeks

### Candidate Drop-Off
- **60%** abandon the process due to length or complexity
- **42%** withdraw because scheduling takes too long
- **47%** cite poor communication as a reason for dropping out
- **40%** report being ghosted after a second or third round
- **32%** of withdrawn candidates accepted another offer from a different company
- **Half of all employers** have lost quality talent due to poor interview process

### Candidate Perception
- **32%** say 2-3 rounds is already too many
- **52%** say 4-5 rounds is too many

> Sources: JobScore Interview Statistics 2025; High5Test Interview Statistics

## Better Methods Exist But Are Underused

| Method | Predictive Validity | Current Usage |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| Structured work samples | .54 | Low |
| Structured interviews | .51 | Moderate |
| Cognitive ability tests | .51 | Low-Moderate |
| Unstructured interviews | .38 | Very High (default) |
| Years of experience | .16 | Very High (default) |
| Age | .00 | Used as proxy |

**Work samples are the single highest-validity selection method** -- and the least used.
DevStage engagements ARE extended work samples.

### Data-Driven Approaches
- Predictive job matching systems achieve **85-92% accuracy**
- Data-driven hiring reduces time-to-hire by **25-40%**
- Skills-based hiring improves retention by **30-50%**
- Strong structured onboarding improves retention by **82%** and productivity by **70%+**

## The Irony

Companies spend $18K-$36K on recruiting and 4-7 weeks on interviews,
using a method that works **57% of the time**. Meanwhile, work sample tests
(the highest-validity method) are rarely used because they're "too expensive."
