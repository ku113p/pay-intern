# Target Audience: Budget-Constrained Startups

## Profile

Small companies and startups that need developers to survive but can't afford
traditional recruiting costs or compete with big tech compensation.

**Who they are:**
- Bootstrapped founders (99.95% of startups never raise VC -- Fundera)
- Early-stage funded startups watching burn rate
- Small agencies and dev shops scaling up
- Solo founders looking for first technical hire

**Defining constraints:**
- Cannot afford $18K-$36K recruiting agency fees
- Offer 11% lower base salaries than big tech
- Cannot invest 40+ hours in each hiring cycle
- A single bad hire can be existential

## Key Statistics

### Hiring Challenge Scale
- **46%** of new hires fail within 18 months (Leadership IQ)
- **74%** of small business employers have made at least one bad hire
- **23%** of startups fail specifically due to wrong team composition
- Average startup cost-per-hire: **~$12,000** (higher per-hire than enterprise due to lack of scale)

> Source: Leadership IQ; ManpowerGroup; CB Insights

### Resource Constraints
- Founders spend **40%** of working hours on non-income tasks like hiring
- **30-40 hours** invested per hire over 4-8 weeks
- Empty technical seats cost **2x annual salary** in lost productivity
- **76%** of employers struggle to fill roles (ManpowerGroup 2025)

### The Funding Reality
- Only **0.05%** of startups raise VC (Fundera)
- VC funding declined **30%** in Q1 2024
- New grad hiring at startups down **11%** from 2023

## Current Alternatives (and Why They Fail)

| Alternative | Why It Fails for Startups |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Recruiting agencies | $18K-$36K per hire -- often exceeds monthly runway |
| Job boards | Drowned in 200+ applications, no quality signal |
| Referral networks | Limited reach, exhausted quickly |
| Freelance platforms | 20-55% markups, no long-term fit evaluation |
| DIY hiring | Founders spend 40% of time on it instead of product |

## What They Want

1. **Low-cost talent evaluation** -- see real work without agency fees
2. **Risk reduction** -- try before committing to full salary
3. **Speed** -- find capable developers without months of searching
4. **Alignment check** -- cultural fit and work style compatibility
5. **Potential pipeline** -- ongoing access to motivated developers

## How DevStage Solves It

- **Zero agency fees** -- post a listing with outcome criteria, review applications
- **Try-before-you-hire** -- evaluate through real work, not expensive interview gauntlets
- **Bidirectional value** -- developers seeking experience are highly motivated, unlike passive candidates
- **Structured evaluation** -- outcome criteria provide objective basis for hiring decisions
- **Converts to hires** -- companies like Automattic achieve 96% retention with work-trial hires

## Messaging Angles

- **Primary**: "Find developers without the $36K recruiting bill."
- **ROI-focused**: "Your first hire shouldn't cost more than your monthly runway."
- **Risk-focused**: "A bad first hire kills 23% of startups. See real work before you commit."
- **Empathy**: "We know you can't compete with Google salaries. You can compete with real projects."

## Monetization Angle

Some startups can actually **generate revenue** through DevStage by offering structured
mentorship/internship programs. Developers pay companies for the opportunity to work on
real projects -- turning the hiring cost into a revenue stream.
