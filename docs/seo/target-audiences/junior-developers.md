# Target Audience: Junior Developers

The largest and most underserved segment. Juniors face a structural crisis in 2024-2026
that makes breaking into tech harder than at any point in the past two decades.

## Profile

**Who they are:**
- CS graduates (112,720 bachelor's degrees awarded in 2022-2023 -- doubled since 2013)
- Bootcamp graduates (6-12 month job search post-completion)
- Self-taught developers (47% of all developers are self-taught)
- Recent graduates unable to find first tech role

**Defining reality:**
- Face 6.1% unemployment rate (2025) -- higher than fine arts or liberal arts majors
- 41-42.5% underemployment -- working in jobs not requiring a degree
- Average job search: 6 months, 200+ applications

## The Experience Paradox

The core problem DevStage solves for this audience:

```
Need experience to get hired
        |
        v
Need to be hired to get experience
        |
        v
(repeat forever)
```

### The Numbers

- **35-38%** of "entry-level" jobs require 3+ years of experience (LinkedIn, 3.8M postings)
- **True entry-level roles** represent only **~2.5%** of all tech job postings
- Entry-level tech hiring fell **73%** between 2023 and 2024
- New graduates represented **32%** of Big Tech hires in 2019; by 2026, only **7%** (78% reduction)
- **Salesforce halted junior hiring entirely** for 2025

> Source: Stanford Digital Economy Lab; IEEE Spectrum; Indeed Hiring Lab; NACE

### The Bait-and-Switch
- "Entry-level software engineer" postings **grew 47%** (Oct 2023-Nov 2024)
- But **actual hiring** into those levels **dropped 73%**
- Companies advertise junior roles, then fill them with experienced engineers

### Internship Pipeline Collapse
- Internship listings fell **30%** since 2023 (Handshake)
- **273 applications** per tech internship (up from 161 the prior year)
- Only **62%** of interns receive full-time offers -- lowest in 5+ years

## What Alternatives Exist (and Why They Fail)

| Alternative | Limitation |
|-------------|-----------|
| Open source | Requires significant unpaid time; no mentorship; hard to find projects |
| Freelancing | Race-to-bottom pricing; 20% platform fees; clients prefer experience |
| Personal projects | AI can generate a to-do app in 60 seconds; basic projects prove nothing |
| Bootcamps | Market flooded; employers skeptical; 6-12 month post-completion search |
| Certificates | Verify theory, not production ability; ATS still filters by experience |

**Core limitation:** None provide **verified team experience on production codebases**.

## Mental Health Impact

- **68%** report "extended silence" from employers during applications (Handshake 2024)
- **60%+** report "overwhelming anxiety" related to career prospects (ACHA 2024)
- **34%** remain concerned about layoffs even during market recovery
- Nearly half complete **unpaid projects** during interviews -- adding exploitation frustration

## What They Want

1. **Real experience** on real codebases with real teams
2. **Verified proof** that they can do the work (not just a certificate)
3. **Mentorship** from working professionals
4. **A way in** that doesn't require 3 years of experience they don't have
5. **Respect** -- not to be treated as disposable free labor

## How DevStage Solves It

| Problem | DevStage Solution |
|---------|-------------------|
| No experience | Work on real projects with real companies |
| No proof of ability | Structured outcome reviews (pass/partial/fail) |
| No mentorship | Work with company teams who set criteria and guide work |
| No way in | Apply to listings based on motivation, not years of experience |

## The Long-Term Industry Argument

Today's juniors are tomorrow's senior engineers and tech leads:
- A **67% hiring cliff in 2024-2026** means **67% fewer potential leaders in 2031-2036**
- The U.S. faces a projected shortage of **1.2 million software engineers by 2026**
- BLS projects **15% growth** in developer employment from 2024-2034

DevStage helps rebuild the pipeline that traditional hiring has destroyed.
