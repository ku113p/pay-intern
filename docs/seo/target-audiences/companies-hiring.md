# Target Audience: Companies Frustrated with Hiring

## Profile

Companies (from startups to enterprises) struggling with the cost, time, and unreliability
of traditional developer hiring processes.

**Who they are:**
- Engineering managers, CTOs, VPs of Engineering
- HR leaders and talent acquisition teams
- Founders wearing the "hiring hat"

**Current behavior:**
- Post on job boards ($200-$500 per listing)
- Pay recruiting agencies 15-25% of first-year salary ($18K-$36K)
- Run 4-6 interview rounds over 4-7 weeks
- Still make bad hires 74% of the time (CareerBuilder)

## Key Pain Points

### 1. Extreme Cost
- Average cost-per-hire: **$4,700** (SHRM 2024), up 17.5% year-over-year
- Agency fees for developers: **$18,000-$36,000** per hire
- Total first-year cost with onboarding: **$130,000-$180,000+**
- Cost of vacancy: **$500/day** in lost productivity

> Source: SHRM Benchmarking Report 2024; Dover Tech Recruiter Fees Guide 2025

### 2. Resume Fraud Epidemic
- **55%** of IT employees admit to resume falsification (Crosschq 2025)
- **60%** of candidates claim mastery in skills they have only basic knowledge of
- **48%** use unauthorized AI tools during coding interviews
- **17%** of hiring managers encountered deepfake interviews by end of 2024

> Source: Crosschq 2025; FabricHQ State of Cheating 2026; CNBC/Google AI Interview Report

### 3. Interview Inefficiency
- Unstructured interviews predict success only **57% of the time**
- **60%** of candidates abandon the process due to length
- **50%** of employers have lost quality talent due to poor interview process
- "Years of experience" is among the **worst predictors** of job performance

> Source: Schmidt & Hunter meta-analysis; JobScore Interview Statistics 2025

### 4. Bad Hire Risk
- Average cost of bad developer hire: **$150,000-$300,000**
- **20%** of turnover occurs within first 45 days
- Top reason for early departure: "misalignment between job expectations and reality" (30.3%)
- **30%** drop in team morale following a bad hire

> Source: Apollo Technical; DistantJob; SHRM Onboarding Statistics

## What They Want

1. **See candidates work before committing** -- not presentations, not puzzles, real work
2. **Reduce risk** -- measurable evaluation, not gut feelings
3. **Cut costs** -- eliminate agency fees and wasted interview cycles
4. **Speed** -- faster path from "need developer" to "developer producing"

## How DevStage Solves It

| Problem | Current Approach | DevStage Approach |

| Evaluating skills | Resume + interviews (57% accuracy) | Real project work with outcome criteria |
