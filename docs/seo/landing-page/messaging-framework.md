# Messaging Framework

## Core Positioning

> **"Work speaks louder than resumes."**

This statement differentiates DevStage from:
- Exclusivity plays ("top 3%" -- Toptal)
- Cost plays ("save 80%" -- Arc)
- Assessment plays ("skills > pedigree" -- HackerRank)

DevStage centers on **real work as proof**.

## PAS Framework Applied (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

### For Companies

**Problem**: "Hiring developers takes months. You post on job boards, screen hundreds
of resumes, run weeks of interviews -- and still end up with a bad fit."

**Agitate**: "Every week without the right developer costs $3,500 in lost productivity.
Your competitors are shipping while you're still interviewing. And 55% of the resumes
you're reading contain lies."

**Solution**: "DevStage lets you evaluate developers through real work with measurable
outcome criteria. See what they can actually build before you commit."

### For Developers

**Problem**: "You can't get hired without experience. You can't get experience without
a job. 200 applications, zero callbacks."

**Agitate**: "Your bootcamp certificate isn't opening doors. Your personal projects
look like everyone else's. AI can generate a to-do app in 60 seconds. What makes
you different?"

**Solution**: "DevStage connects you with real companies working on real projects.
You get verified outcome reviews that prove what no resume can -- that you can
actually do the work."

## Audience-Specific Copy Directions

### Companies (blue, professional, ROI-focused)
- Tone: Confident, data-driven, business-oriented
- Keywords: "evaluate", "criteria", "real work", "hire with confidence", "reduce risk"
- Emotional trigger: Fear of bad hires, frustration with process

### Junior Developers (green, empathetic, hopeful)
- Tone: Understanding, motivating, practical
- Keywords: "break the loop", "real experience", "verified proof", "your chance"
- Emotional trigger: Frustration with paradox, hope for a way in

### Challenge Seekers (indigo, bold, provocative)
- Tone: Direct, competitive, slightly provocative
- Keywords: "prove it", "real challenges", "production code", "not a sandbox"
- Emotional trigger: Boredom, desire for recognition

### Career Switchers (green, transformational)
- Tone: Supportive, transformative, practical
- Keywords: "from tutorials to production", "prove your skills", "new career"
- Emotional trigger: Uncertainty about new path, desire for validation
