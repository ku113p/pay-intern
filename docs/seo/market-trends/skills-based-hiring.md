# Market Trend: Skills-Based Hiring

## The Movement

Skills-based hiring -- evaluating candidates on demonstrated abilities rather than
degrees, credentials, or years of experience -- has massive stated adoption
but almost zero actual implementation.

## The Intent vs. Execution Gap

- **85%** of companies claim to practice skills-based hiring
- **Harvard/Burning Glass (2024)**: Only **0.14%** of hires are actually impacted
  -- fewer than 1 in 700
- Companies rewrite job postings but **don't change evaluation methods**
- The intent is there; the **infrastructure is not**

> Source: Harvard Business School / Burning Glass Institute 2024

## Degree Requirements Are Falling

- **52%** of US job postings have no education requirement
- **45%** of companies have formally eliminated degree requirements for some roles (SHRM 2024)
- **14 US states** have removed degree requirements for state government jobs
- **81%** of employers now prefer skills over formal degrees (up from 56% in 2022)

But: ATS systems still filter by degrees as proxies. Removing the requirement on
the posting doesn't remove the bias in the process.

## Government Mandates

- **Chance to Compete Act (2024)**: US federal legislation promoting skills-based hiring
- **Merit Hiring Plan (2025)**: White House executive order
- **21+ state initiatives** creating demand for skills-verification infrastructure
- **100,000 federal IT jobs** transitioning to skills-based hiring

## Why Execution Lags Behind Intent

1. **No infrastructure**: Companies removed degree requirements but didn't build alternative evaluation methods
2. **ATS inertia**: Automated screening still uses proxies like degrees and experience years
3. **Manager habits**: Individual hiring managers still default to resume-based filtering
4. **Assessment limitations**: Existing tools test knowledge, not real-world capability
5. **No portable standard**: Each company's definition of "skills-based" is different

## What the Market Needs

A platform that:
- Provides **structured skills evaluation** (not just removal of degree requirements)
- Uses **real work** as the assessment method (highest predictive validity)
- Creates **portable, verified credentials** based on actual performance
- Is accessible to **both companies and developers**
