# Market Trend: Outcome-Based Assessment

## The Research

Work samples are the single highest-validity method for predicting job performance,
yet the least commonly used.

## Predictive Validity Comparison

| Method | Validity | Usage |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Work sample tests** | **.54** | **Rare** |
| Structured interviews | .51 | Moderate |
| Cognitive ability tests | .51 | Low-Moderate |
| Unstructured interviews | .38 | Very High (default) |
| Years of experience | .16 | Very High (default) |
| Reference checks | .26 | Common |

> Source: Schmidt & Hunter 1998 meta-analysis; Sackett et al. 2022 update

**Work samples predict success 42% better than traditional interviews.**

## Structured Assessment Results

Companies that switched to structured, outcome-based assessment:
- **Signpost**: 70% reduction in turnover
- **Clean MD**: 53% reduction in turnover, $45,500 savings
- **Overall**: Structured assessment-driven hiring reduces turnover **41-70%**

> Source: SSR Predictive Hiring Framework 2026; Various case studies

## The Digital Credentials Boom

- Digital credentials market: **$265M** (2024), projected to **$970M** by 2030
- Over **1 million** badges exist in the US
- But most verify **course completion**, not actual work output
- Performance-based exams (CNCF Kubernetes, AWS solutions architect) gaining traction
- Industry consensus: "Certs get past HR filters, but your GitHub portfolio gets the job"

**Gap**: No credential verifies that a developer can work on a REAL production codebase
at a REAL company and deliver results. DevStage fills this gap.

## The "Show Don't Tell" Movement

- **66%** of developers prefer real-world evaluation over theoretical tests
- **46%** say take-home projects best express their abilities
- The era of "tell me about a time when..." is giving way to "show me what you built"
- CoderPad CEO describes this as "the death of algorithmic take-homes --
  replaced by project-based ones"

## AI Is Changing Assessment

- AI can generate code solutions, making traditional coding tests unreliable
- Companies need assessment methods that **can't be cheated**
- Real work over weeks/months is inherently resistant to AI-assisted cheating
- The shift is from "can you write this function" to "can you ship this feature"

## DevStage as Outcome-Based Assessment Infrastructure

DevStage's outcome criteria system is the first **platform-level implementation**
of outcome-based assessment for developer hiring:

1. **Pre-defined criteria**: Company specifies measurable outcomes before work begins
2. **Real work context**: Developer works on actual codebase, not a sandbox
3. **Structured review**: Each criterion rated pass/partial/fail
4. **Overall recommendation**: ready_to_hire / needs_practice / not_recommended
5. **Developer consent**: Developer controls whether review is public
6. **Portable record**: Outcome reviews travel with the developer across opportunities
