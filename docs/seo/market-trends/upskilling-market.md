# Market Trend: The Upskilling Market

## Market Size

- **U.S. training expenditures**: $102.8 billion in 2025
- **Per-employee average**: $1,254/year
- **Individual developer spend**: $0-$2,000/year on education
- **EdTech market**: $163.5 billion, growing to $348 billion by 2030
- **Gamification in education**: $1.14B (2024), projected $18.6B by 2033

## The Completion Crisis

Online courses have fundamentally broken completion rates:

- **Free MOOCs**: 5-15% completion rate
- **90%** of learners quit within the first month
- Even paid courses: 3-15% completion
- BUT: Programs with **accountability and community** reach **70-85%** completion (5-10x improvement)

**Key insight**: The problem isn't motivation -- it's the format.
Self-paced, isolated video courses don't work.
Structured, accountable, community-based learning does.

## Learning Preferences

- **87%** of developers prefer learning by doing over watching
- Experienced developers almost never use video tutorials (**2%** frequent use)
- **"Tutorial hell"** is a documented phenomenon -- completing tutorials
  without being able to build independently
- PBL (project-based learning) groups score **36%** higher than control groups

## What Developers Want to Learn

### High-Demand Technologies (2024-2026)
- **AI/ML**: role postings up 163% year-over-year
- **Rust**: 15-20% salary premiums
- **TypeScript**: continued growth as primary web language
- **Cloud/DevOps**: AWS, Kubernetes, Terraform
- **Security**: role postings up 124% year-over-year

### Skill Obsolescence Anxiety
- **40%** of tech workers predict skills obsolete within 3 years
- **74%** worry AI will make daily skills obsolete
- **96%** prioritize AI upskilling for job security
- **46%** fear skills irrelevant within 5 years

## The Mentorship Gap

- **76%** say mentors are important for career development
- **54%** lack access to any mentor
- Workers with mentors are **2x more likely** to stay 5+ years
- Career changers are the **#1 mentee segment** (30% of all seekers)
- Existing mentorship platforms don't involve real work
